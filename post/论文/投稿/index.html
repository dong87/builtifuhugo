  • What is the scientific question you are addressing?
  • (1) Estimate genetic parameters such as variance estimation and heritability, (2) determine the impact of reciprocal diallel crosses on estimation of combining ability, variance components, and heritability, and (3) investigate heterosis, selfing depression, and correlation between the genetic distance of parents and heterosis for growth traits with EST-SSR markers.
  • What are the key findings that answer this question?
  • (1) Effects of general combining ability (GCA) and special combining ability (SCA) were statistically significant for all analyzed traits. SCAs were more important than GCAs at a young age, while its importance decreased at an older age except for diameter at breast height. Narrow and broad sense heritabilities were fairly low (0.01-0.08 and 0.04-0.14), (2) The reciprocal effect was significant for tree height at an older age only. Variances and heritabilities of GCA and SCA were overestimated without reciprocal matings. While ranking of best linear unbiased prediction (BLUP) breeding values of parents were little affected by reciprocal crosses, which suggested half diallel is capable for a progeny test, (3) Superior mating combinations with significant heterosis were identified. Selfings did not showed severe selfing depression. No significant correlation was observed between SCA estimates or heterosis and genetic distance of parents.
  • Why is this work important and timely?
  • Japanese larch (Larix kaempferi) is one of the most economically important tree species used for the production of both wood and pulp. The results of this study would assist us immensely in a better understanding of genetic variation pattern, selection of a most appropriate breeding mating design and improvement of breeding efficiency.
  • Why is it of interest to the international readership of JOURNAL?
  • (1) Few studies have focused on genetic analysis of a progeny test derived from a full diallel mating design (including selfs) in forest trees, while there has been an increasing interest in inbreeding depression among forest geneticists, tree breeders and forest managers. (2) Tree breeders always concern strongly whether reciprocal crosses should be included in a full diallel because it is difficult to harvest sufficient seeds from the full diallel mating design for various reasons. In this study demostrated that reciparocal effect was negligible and is capable for a progeny test. (3) Japanese larch (Larix kaempferi) is one of the most economically important tree species in the world. But few genetic improvement studies in this species have been published. The results of this paper may be of great interest to those genetists and breeders with interest in genetic improvement of larch and other tree species.
